            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p><em>Update: migration timeline is now from December 8th to February 18th</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Autodesk Platform Services (APS) has launched a new business model for cloud APIs, making access more flexible and predictable so you can plan with confidence as your projects grow.&nbsp; The changes will impact all customers and developers using APS APIs so please read on for two critical steps you’ll need to take as this transition begins.&nbsp;</p>
<p>APS APIs are powering more of your work than ever—driving design automation, intelligent workflows, and connected experiences. To make sure you can keep innovating with confidence, we’re introducing a new business model for cloud APIs at the end of the year.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h1>Key Takeaways&nbsp;</h1>
<p>The new model is built around three core benefits:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Accessibility</strong>: Ongoing access to experiment with APIs and explore the right tools for your projects, with monthly usage caps that let you try paid APIs before you scale.&nbsp;</li>
<li><strong>Flexibility</strong>: Choose from payment options that align with your usage, so costs stay predictable as your needs evolve.&nbsp;</li>
<li><strong>Innovation: </strong>A modernized way to access and pay for APIs that lays the foundation for Agentic AI and model context protocol servers (MCPs), enabling “describe-and-do” workflows of the future.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p>With a simple, two-tier access structure the new business model is designed to give you transparency into API usage, predictable costs, and the ability to adapt as your projects grow.&nbsp;</p>
<p>To continue using APS APIs once the new model launches, you’ll need to<strong> take two key actions:&nbsp;</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Choose an offering under the new two-tier model:</strong> Free or Paid. Most APS API users will start in Free.&nbsp;If your needs go beyond the usage caps for paid APIs in the Free tier, you can move to a Paid option. More information about each offering is detailed below.&nbsp;</li>
<li><strong>Set up and <a href="https://aps.autodesk.com/blog/how-create-developer-hub-and-migrate-your-applications">migrate your apps to a developer hub</a>. </strong>This ensures your API usage is tracked correctly and billing applies to the right team.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p>You'll have a window after rollout to complete these steps. If they aren’t completed by <strong>February 18</strong>, your API access will be suspended until you’ve enrolled in an offer and migrated your apps. You can also prepare now by purchasing Flex tokens for paid APIs and setting up a developer hub in advance of the launch.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Here is a closer look at what’s changing and what to expect before the end of the year.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h1>A new two-tier business model&nbsp;</h1>
<p>APS is moving to a simpler business model with two tiers of access: Free and Paid. The new model makes it easy to try our APIs, track your activity, and pay only when your needs grow.&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Free tier&nbsp;</h2>
<p>The Free tier provides monthly access to cloud APIs. This offering is ideal for developers to test, learn, and build proofs of concept (POCs) and for customers with product subscriptions to access their product APIs. It gives you a way to start experimenting while gaining visibility into usage.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<div>
<p><strong>How it works:</strong>&nbsp;Automation API, Model Derivative API, Flow Graph Engine API, and Reality Capture API are rated, which means they have limited amounts of usage available each month. The Free tier is available to you as long as you are using non-rated APIs and stay under monthly usage caps for rated APIs. If you need to exceed monthly limits for rated APIs you will need to sign up for one of the Paid tiers to continue usage.</p>
<p style="margin: 0;"><img alt="Free Tier" height="166" src="https://aps.autodesk.com/sites/default/files/inline-images/Screenshot%202025-09-11%20at%202.00.32%E2%80%AFPM.png" width="293" /></p>
</div>
<h2><strong>Paid tier &nbsp;</strong></h2>
<p>If your usage needs go beyond the Free tier, you can move to the Paid tier and choose from two payment options:&nbsp;</p>
<div>
<div>
<h4>Prepay (with Flex)</h4>
<p><b>How it works:</b>&nbsp;Similar to how Flex works today, you purchase tokens in advance to pay for API usage. This option provides predictability and better control over your budget. Flex tokens are consumed first whenever you use APIs. If you run out, your access is suspended until you purchase more Flex tokens or are also enrolled in Pay as You Go.&nbsp;</p>
</div>
<p style="margin: 0;"><img alt="PrePay" height="177" src="https://aps.autodesk.com/sites/default/files/inline-images/Screenshot%202025-09-11%20at%202.00.37%E2%80%AFPM.png" width="310" /></p>
</div>
<div>
<div>
<h4>Pay as You Go&nbsp;</h4>
<p><b>How it works:</b>&nbsp;A new payment option that lets you use cloud APIs without upfront commitments. It’s designed for flexibility and lets you keep working without worrying about Flex tokens running out.&nbsp;You are&nbsp;billed monthly based on your actual usage,&nbsp;with flexibility to use our APIs as much or as little as you need. Availability varies by region.&nbsp;</p>
</div>
<p style="margin: 0;"><img alt="Pay as You Go" height="184" src="https://aps.autodesk.com/sites/default/files/inline-images/Screenshot%202025-09-11%20at%202.00.42%E2%80%AFPM.png" /></p>
</div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><em>Cloud credits transition: By the end of this year, APS will also stop accepting cloud credits as payment and will officially transition to Flex. To avoid disruption and maintain access to APS APIs, you’ll need to purchase Flex tokens. If you need support during this transition, please reach out to <a href="https://www.autodesk.com/support">Autodesk support.</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Paid API Pricing Under New Model &nbsp;</h2>
<p>APS cloud APIs will move to the new business model at the end of the year, including the four existing paid APIs: <strong>Automation API, Model Derivative API, Flow Graph Engine API, and Reality Capture API</strong>. Each paid API will include a limited amount of usage every month within the Free tier, with the option to purchase a Paid tier offering if more usage is needed.&nbsp;In addition, pricing for the <strong>Model Derivative API and Automation API for Revit, AutoCAD, and Inventor</strong> will increase to better reflect the value they provide. Some activities that were previously free will now be billed. See the chart below for a breakdown of the new pricing.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p><!--%3Cmeta%20charset%3D%22UTF-8%22%20%2F%3E--></p>
<p><img alt="API Rate Chart 11/12/2025" src="https://aps.autodesk.com/sites/default/files/inline-images/API%20Rate%20Chart_11-12-25-1.jpg" /></p>
<p>All other APS APIs will not be rated (charged for use) in the December launch. You can view a full list of non-rated (free) APS APIs <a href="https://aps.autodesk.com/pricing">here</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Your path forward with APS&nbsp;</h2>
<p>To ensure continuity, make sure you sign up for an offer that fits your needs and that your apps have been moved to a developer hub once the new model launches. You will have a window after rollout to complete these steps, but you can also act early by purchasing Flex tokens for paid APIs moving to the new model and migrating apps to a hub to help ensure a seamless transition. &nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>Support is available throughout this transition. You’ll find resources on the <a href="https://aps.autodesk.com/get-help">APS Get Help page</a>, including <a href="https://aps.autodesk.com/developer/documentation">documentation</a>, <a href="https://github.com/autodesk-platform-services">GitHub repos</a>, and <a href="https://stackoverflow.com/questions/ask?tags=autodesk-platform-services">Stack Overflow resources</a>. <a href="https://aps.autodesk.com/adn">Autodesk Developer Network</a> (ADN) members can also access one-on-one support through their membership.&nbsp;</p>

<h1>&nbsp;</h1>
<p><a class="anchor" id="faqs"></a></p>
<h1>FAQs&nbsp;</h1>
<p>We know a shift like this comes with questions. That’s why we’ve included answers below to help you understand what’s changing and what it means for your work.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>What is changing?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Autodesk Platform Services (APS) is moving to a new business model and pricing structure:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>All APS APIs will be offered under a new two-tier model starting in December. You must enroll in one of these offerings to continue using Autodesk Platform Service APIs.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
<li>There will be four APIs that are rated when the business model launches: Automation API, Model Derivative API, Flow Graph Engine API, and Reality Capture API. Each will include a limited amount of usage every month in the free tier, with the option to purchase more as needed.&nbsp;</li>
<li>In addition, pricing for the Model Derivative API and Automation API for Revit, AutoCAD, and Inventor will change to better reflect the value they provide.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<h4>When will these changes go into effect?</h4>
<p>The new business model, as well as pricing updates, will go into effect at the December 8, 2025.</p>
<h4>I am not using any of the paid APIs. Do I still need to sign up for an offer?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Yes, all cloud API users will need to take action by signing up for a tiered offering when the new business model launches. If you do not plan to use any paid APIs, you will still need to acquire the Free Tier offering for your Team, set up a developer hub within that Team, then move your app to that developer hub.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>What happens if I don’t sign up for an offer?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>You will have until February 18 to sign up for an offer after the change takes effect. Once that period ends, your applications will no longer work until you sign up and complete the subsequent onboarding steps.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>What do I need to do to be ready?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Here’s how to prepare so your apps keep running smoothly:&nbsp;</p>
<ol>
<li>If you use any APIs that are in the Paid tier, purchase Flex tokens in advance to avoid service interruption.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
<li>Review the two tiers and payment options so you are ready to sign up when the model goes live. &nbsp;</li>
<li>When the new model becomes available, you’ll need to <a href="https://aps.autodesk.com/blog/how-create-developer-hub-and-migrate-your-applications">create a developer hub</a> and migrate any existing apps to it. Instructions can be found in <a href="https://aps.autodesk.com/blog/how-create-developer-hub-and-migrate-your-applications">this blog</a>.</li>
</ol>
<h4>Is the Free tier limited in time?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>No. It’s available each month, but you must stay under the monthly usage caps for paid APIs.</p>
<h4>What are the usage caps for paid APIs?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>You can view usage caps for paid APIs in the rate table above.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>What happens if I go over the Free tier cap?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>If you exceed the threshold on the Free tier for one of the four paid APIs (Automation API, Model Derivative API, Flow Graph Engine API, and Reality Capture API), your usage for that API will be suspended until the following month or you can opt into the Paid tier.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>When I opt into the Paid tier, do I still get free monthly usage caps for paid APIs?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>No. Once you’re on a Paid tier, usage of paid APIs is metered and billed based on actual consumption. &nbsp;</p>
<h4>Will the Free tier replace the 90 day trial for APS?&nbsp;&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Yes.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<h4>How can I monitor API usage in the Free tier?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>A record of your monthly usage will be visible directly in developer hubs.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>What is a Flex token?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Flex tokens are a unit of measurement used to access APS APIs and select Autodesk products. For example, they may be consumed when translating a file in a cloud environment. You can learn more about Flex tokens at <a href="https://www.autodesk.com/buying/flex">autodesk.com/flex.</a>&nbsp;</p>
<h4>Is there a minimum purchase for Flex tokens?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Yes. The minimum purchase is 100 tokens.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>Do Flex tokens expire?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Yes. Tokens expire one year from the purchase date.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>Are volume discounts available?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Yes. Discounts are available based on how many Flex tokens you purchase. For more information on Flex tokens visit autodesk.com/flex.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>What happens if I run out of Flex tokens?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>If you’re signed up for Pay as You Go in addition to Prepay, billing continues monthly. If not, your access will be suspended until more tokens are purchased.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>How is Pay as You Go different from Prepay?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>With Prepay, you buy Flex tokens in advance, and Flex tokens are consumed as you use. With Pay as You Go, you’re billed monthly for your actual usage.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>Who is eligible for Pay as You Go?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>At launch, Pay as You Go will be available to eligible customers in select countries.*&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<h4>Can I use Pay as You Go without Flex?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Yes. You can enroll in Pay as You Go only.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>If I have both Flex and Pay as You Go, which is used first?&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Flex tokens are always consumed first. After tokens run out, billing moves to Pay as You Go.&nbsp;</p>
<p><em>*Available to eligible customers in the US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, European Economic Area, UK and Switzerland, paying with a credit card or PayPal, and in good standing.&nbsp;</em></p>
<h4>How are Basic Interactions for the Model Derivative API defined?</h4>
<p>Along with the price adjustments for translating Complex or Simple files with the Model Derivative API, additional interactions with the API will be charged at the rate of 1 token for every 300,000 API calls. This included all calls to endpoints under the Model Derivative API that don't directly trigger a Complex or Simple translation job. We are calling these "Basic Interactions". A list of requests and endpoints that fall under this category are listed below:</p>
<p>GET&nbsp;/formats&nbsp;<br />
GET /{urn}/manifest&nbsp;<br />
DELETE /{urn}/manifest&nbsp;<br />
GET /{urn}/manifest/{derivativeUrn}/signedcookies&nbsp;<br />
HEAD /{urn}/manifest/{derivativeUrn}&nbsp;<br />
GET /{urn}/thumbnail&nbsp;<br />
GET /{urn}/metadata&nbsp;<br />
GET /{urn}/metadata/{modelGuid}&nbsp;<br />
GET /{urn}/metadata/{modelGuid}/properties&nbsp;</p>
