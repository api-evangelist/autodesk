            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p>AI makes it easier than ever to turn ideas into something real. But in design and engineering, moving from intent to an editable, manufacturable design still takes time and manual work.&nbsp;</p>
<p>At Autodesk, our approach to AI is&nbsp;simple:&nbsp;help move work forward—whether that happens inside our products or in the tools our customers already use.&nbsp;</p>
<p>With Fusion, that looks like two things:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Autodesk Assistant&nbsp;brings AI directly into Fusion,&nbsp;helping users&nbsp;understand context and take action in their workflows&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Fusion Model Context Protocols (MCPs)&nbsp;lets&nbsp;third-party AI systems connect to Fusion, enabling them to access design context and perform actions securely.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Today’s announcement builds on that second path&nbsp;–&nbsp;bringing Fusion into Claude through&nbsp;MCP&nbsp;as part of Anthropic's Claude for Creative Work launch.</p>
<p>Autodesk Fusion MCP connects Claude directly to the Fusion environment&nbsp;for Fusion subscribers,&nbsp;so ideas can move beyond the prompt and into real, structured design work.</p>
<p><a class="button button--default button--medium" href="https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-creative-work">View Anthropic announcement</a></p>
<h4>Design, inside the conversation&nbsp;</h4>
<p>People already use tools like Claude to explore ideas, solve problems, and write code.&nbsp;Now, design&nbsp;is&nbsp;part&nbsp;of that same workflow.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Unlike text or code, design work is&nbsp;built&nbsp;on geometry, constraints&nbsp;and relationships.&nbsp;The Fusion MCP&nbsp;gives Claude guided access to that structure,&nbsp;so&nbsp;users&nbsp;can participate&nbsp;in the design process while execution remains securely within Fusion.&nbsp;</p>
<p>With the Autodesk Fusion MCP, you can:&nbsp;<br />
• Turn natural language into design actions&nbsp;<br />
• Iterate without starting from scratch&nbsp;<br />
• Automate repetitive modeling steps&nbsp;<br />
• Move faster from idea to manufacturable output</p>
<div class="video-embed-field-provider-youtube video-embed-field-responsive-video">
</div>

<h4>From ideas to execution&nbsp;</h4>
<p>AI is powerful at pattern recognition and coordination,&nbsp;but generating an idea is not the same as producing something real.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Manufacturable outcome requires engineering rigor, precision, and&nbsp;domain&nbsp;data and&nbsp;expertise&nbsp;built over decades. That level of fidelity remains grounded in Fusion.&nbsp;</p>
<p>As an open standard,&nbsp;MCPs&nbsp;bridge&nbsp;the two: letting&nbsp;AI&nbsp;understand context and take action across tools&nbsp;safely, with defined boundaries&nbsp;so&nbsp;you can move from idea to execution without switching contexts.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<h4>Built with your data in mind&nbsp;</h4>
<p>Connecting Fusion&nbsp;with Claude&nbsp;doesn’t change how your data is handled.&nbsp;</p>
<p>You stay in control of&nbsp;what’s accessed&nbsp;and how it’s used, with protections aligned to Autodesk’s privacy, security, and product standards.&nbsp;</p>
<h4>Looking ahead&nbsp;</h4>
<p>This is an early step&nbsp;in a&nbsp;broader shift toward a more open ecosystem where Autodesk software connects into the tools our customers already use.&nbsp;</p>
<p>We’re working toward a world where:&nbsp;<br />
• Design tools are accessible from anywhere you work&nbsp;<br />
• AI can safely take action inside real workflows&nbsp;<br />
• Ideas move from concept to production without friction&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;Autodesk&nbsp;Fusion is there wherever it begins.</p>
